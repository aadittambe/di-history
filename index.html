1868

The University Reporter begins publishing as a 16-page monthly student newspaper on the University of Iowa campus.

1878

The Vidette, a rival student newspaper, begins publishing.

1881

The University Reporter and The Vidette merge to become The Vidette-Reporter, a tri-weekly campus publication.

1918

Mildred Whitcomb, of Ottumwa, Iowa, is named editor of The Daily Iowan, becoming the newspaper’s first female editor and one of the first women to head an American college daily newspaper. She is shown below seated at the desk to the right in a 1919 photo.

1922

Iowa native George Gallup, creator of the Gallup Poll, attends the University of Iowa and serves as editor of The Daily Iowan. He earns a B.A. in 1923, an M.A. in 1925, and a Ph.D. in 1928. He is pictured surrounded by his staff in the Daily Iowan offices.

1923

The Daily Iowan becomes the first college newspaper to feature full Associated Press wire service.

1924

The University of Iowa establishes the School of Journalism and Mass Communication. It is headquartered alongside The Daily Iowan in Close Hall, which was all but destroyed by fire in 1940 and demolished in the late 1960s.

1945

Dottie Ray, later recognized as one of the first women to host her own radio talk show, served as editor of the first all-female staff for The Daily Iowan in 1944-45. She is pictured below seated third from the left.

1953

The Daily Iowan moves to the Communications Center. Shown below are students working in the newsroom in a photo from the 1954 Hawkeye yearbook.

1962

The Daily Iowan is honored for excellence by the Inland Daily Press Association. Pictured: Editor-in-chief Phil Currie and Publisher Fred Pownall review the winning issue of The Daily Iowan near the trophy.

1976

The Daily Iowan is named Newspaper of the Year by the Iowa Newspaper Association. It earns the honor again in 1981.

1987

The Daily Iowan scholarship program (later named after Bill Mertens) is established to attract the best high school journalists to the University of Iowa—and to the newsroom. The DI will reach $1 million in scholarships awarded this fall.

2000

The Daily Iowan earns its first of five Pacemaker awards, the college newspaper equivalent of the Pulitzer Prize, from the Associated Collegiate Press. The paper wins subsequent awards in 2001, 2006, 2008, and 2013.

2005

The Daily Iowan moves into the Adler Journalism Building.

2017

Bill Casey retires after being publisher of The Daily Iowan for 41 years. In 2005, he was awarded Iowa Newspaper Association Master Editor-Publisher, one of the most prestigious awards given to Iowa newspaper professionals.

2017

The Daily Iowan newsroom receives an extensive renovation that provides a more modern and open workspace. The remodel included the preservation of a collection of historical front pages that showcase some of the most memorable events in U.S. and university history.
